About Us

Statement of Faith

We believe in the Holy Bible, comprising both the Old and New Testaments as the inspired word of God of which the New does not condemn the Old, but the fulfilment of the latter. We believe that the bloodshed, death and resurrection of our Lord and Saviour Jesus Christ for the reconciliation of man to God, is a foundation which the conquest over death and hell by Christ for mankind is based

About The General Overseer

Rev. Dr. Davidson O. Kormoi

General Overseer & Founder

Rev. Davidson O. Kormoi was born in Moyowa in the Jong Chiefdom, Bonthe District, Republic of Sierra Leone. He attended Centennial Secondary School in Mattru Jong and the Kay School in Kenema Respectively. He briefly taught at the Pampana High School and later at the Government Secondary School for Boys in Magburaka.

He worked at the Central Bank of Sierra Leone and retired in a senior Management position. He lectured part time at the Sierra Leone Theological College in Freetown and he is at present Associate lecturer, school of postgraduate studies, Njala University, Freetown and Bo. He is also the founder and General Overseer of the Trumpet of God Evangelical Ministry. He holds a PhD in Business Administration, MBA (Finance), MSc (Agric. Econ), CPS, etc.

Dr. Kormoi is a writer and consultant, the Author of Dark Shadows, and the end of Dark Shadows, memoires now on amazon. He is also the author of “The Central bank of Mendenesia” and “Eight morale boosting short stories for progressive youths and young adults.” He desires that every member of his church is saved, prospers on earth and gains heaven through total submission to Jesus Christ and obeying His commandments.

Our Vision

Our vision is to preach the undiluted and or the uncompromised word of God to all mankind that they would be saved. To help the poor and needy by way of empowerment through the provision of physical, financial and spiritual assistance where applicable

Background of Ministry

In 2003, Christ the King Evangelical Ministry was Established at quarry, back of hotel 510, in a make-shift school compound paying a rent of 300 Leones per annum to the Proprietor of the school. Quarry was a large establishment in the North west corner of Freetown, with a large Muslim population and other faiths. There was a large group of fetish priests, herbalists and Juju mongers in the said community. The practice of Christianity became a threat to all other practitioners of faith and we were considered enemies.

The battle became so strong that the Muslim clerics announced that no land should be sold to us in Quarry. The president of the Herbalists and all Fetish priests Association bought the make shift school, where we were holding services and we were driven from the school. Somebody gave us an unfinished unroofed house where we kept services. We used tarpaulins to cover part of the structure from the rain during services

Shortly after these events, my office, the Bank of Sierra Leone, sent me to Boulder, Colorado, USA for two months to study supervision of Micro Finance Banks and other financial Institutions. It was there I met a man whom I have never known, a man with the heart of God, a white American Citizen, Mr. Leon Palmer. This Man drove us in his car to our university residence and invited us to his house for dinner. We exchanged contacts and after my return to Sierra Leone, I explained to him all that had befallen the church. He asked me, “What do you want me to do?” I told him I needed tarpaulins and sticks to put up a makeshift church. This man sent USD4,810 at intervals and we were able to build the present church in addition to our own resources.

However, we were outwitted by a Judas who purported to be a pastor in the church. He came to the church when I was in America, and seeing our plight he announced that his father-in-law had land which we can use to build our church. I announced to the church that I have received 4,810 dollars for our church. I had a genuine motive but this pastor had a wrong motive. Agreement was reached for me to sacrifice one town plot of land I had in West end of Freetown for their plot of land in quarry, where we should build the church. It was an oral agreement and believing that we were all Christians, it should be a reliable agreement. No sooner had we bought all the materials and assembled them at the site, than the pastor and his father-in-law changed their minds. They said the land was for lease. Sadly, we agreed because we could not take the sand, the blocks and the other materials back and we had no place for storage. Up till now we are paying lease rent for a church house we have built. In 2025, the lease rent was astronomically increased from 2,000 Leones per annum to Le10,000 per annum otherwise we were to be evicted. So, to God be the glory. Our goal is to buy our own land and build our own church, hence an appeal to donors and philanthropists.

In the year 2009, we changed the name of the Church from Christ the King Family to The Trumpet of God Evangelical Ministry by the Inspiration of the Holy Spirit to properly align with our vision based on the scripture in 1st Thessalonians 4:16. It was in Line with the Lords call for end times messages to prepare the minds of people for the coming of the Lord.

Welfare

We cater for the welfare of our members who are vulnerable in specific areas. We cater for coffins, support weddings to avoid co-habitations, help with business capital where extremely necessary to salvage devoted but disadvantaged members, particularly widows and or orphans, or the physically challenged. We also help with medical bills where genuinely necessary. Finally, we maintain a counselling session where advises and disputes are amicably settled

We extend helping hands to the less fortunate in the church, help to pay school and college fees to some students, and of late we are thinking of school dropouts and drug related consequences through counselling and rehab facilities. This would require external assistance by way of donors and well wishers

Our Goals For 2026 - 2030

For 2026, we would like to support 20 students in technical and vocational colleges pursuing technical qualifications in Building and construction courses, Electrical and electronic engineering, Plumbing, Refrigerator engineering, civil engineering, carpentry, automobile engineering all at certificate and Diploma levels.

We would also like to help 40 vulnerable widows with average capital for small and medium enterprises.

We would also like to support 40 orphans in primary and secondary schools. Support depends on the availability of funds. We may include pictures of widows and orphans at their discretion, on our web page to solicit funds from philanthropists and well-wishers who would like to support vulnerable people. Such funds are strictly controlled by our church to avoid abuse, misuse and or scamming.
